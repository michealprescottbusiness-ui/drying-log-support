Common Questions

Does the iPhone measure moisture?

No. Drying Log records readings from your professional moisture meter. Meter-display OCR can suggest a number from a photo, but you must confirm it against the instrument before saving.

Where is job data stored?

Job records and photos are cached on your device and sync through your private iCloud database to devices using the same Apple Account. Drying Log does not operate an account server, cannot view private CloudKit records in the developer portal, and does not receive your job data.

How do I create a report?

Open a job, tap Documents, and choose Final Drying Report, Daily Visit Report, or Claim Summary. You can preview the PDF before sharing it.

How do estimates and invoices work?

Open Create, choose a job, and describe the scope by talking, typing, or scanning a written scope. Drying Log creates an editable estimate or invoice using your saved business defaults and keeps a scanned source image with the job. The finished document opens as a scrollable preview with consolidated editing and Save or Save & Send controls. Review every line, quantity, and rate before sharing. Estimates and invoices are business documents and do not determine insurance coverage or reimbursement.

How do I add company branding?

Open Settings, then Company & Branding. Add the company name, contact details, certification, and logo used on Pro reports.

How do I cancel or restore Pro?

Subscriptions are managed by Apple in Settings > Apple Account > Subscriptions. Use Restore Purchases on the Drying Log upgrade screen after changing devices.

How do I delete data?

Tap Edit or swipe left on a job and confirm Delete. Use Settings > More > Delete All Data to remove every synced job after typing DELETE. Removing the app from one device does not necessarily remove records retained in private iCloud storage.

Protect private job information. Do not attach customer names, addresses, claim documents, or jobsite photos to a public support request.
